How to Choose Essential vs. Non-Essential Equipment

Ask these 3 questions before buying any piece of equipment:

  1. Is it used by 70%+ of members? (e.g., treadmills = yes; leg extension = no)
  2. Does it support multiple training styles? (e.g., cable machines = strength + HIIT + rehab)
  3. Can it generate revenue directly? (e.g., via small-group classes or personal training)

If an item fails any of these, defer it. Focus on **versatility, durability, and member demand**—not aesthetics or trends.

How to Allocate Your Equipment Budget

For a $50,000 startup budget, allocate as follows:

  • 60% Equipment: $30,000 → Focus 80% on the 10 essentials above
  • 20% Layout & Infrastructure: $10,000 → Flooring, mirrors, lighting, paint
  • 15% Marketing: $7,500 → Pre-launch campaigns, member acquisition
  • 5% Contingency: $2,500 → Unexpected shipping or installation costs

Avoid overspending on specialty machines (e.g., glute bridges, neck machines) until you hit 100+ active members.

Common Equipment Mistakes New Gym Owners Make

  • Buying home-grade equipment: It voids insurance and breaks within months.
  • Over-purchasing cardio: 3–5 treadmills are enough for most gyms—members rotate usage.
  • Ignoring maintenance access: Leave 36” behind machines for cleaning and part replacement.
  • Skipping layout planning: Poor flow causes congestion and member frustration.
